Method, interpretation, and citation

How the score works

Within each country's China-import basket, dependence, log10 China import value, and PCI are winsorized at the 1st/99th percentiles and min-max normalized to 0–100. The composite priority score (CPS) is a weighted mean over available indicators.

CPS = Σ(weight × normalized indicator) / Σ(available weights)

Exposure is fixed at 0.6 × normalized dependence + 0.4 × normalized scale. Sophistication is normalized PCI. Threshold equality counts as “high.” A material shortlist item is in quadrant A and exceeds $5 million of China imports.

Reading the data carefully

  • PCI is bridged from HS2012 to HS2022 by exact HS6 where available, then an HS4 fallback. Products with no PCI remain ranked on available indicators but are marked U—not treated as low complexity.
  • 2022–2023 values average reported positive BACI flows. BACI has no explicit zero-flow rows; absent rows are not imputed.
  • Chapter dependence covers products with observed China imports, because zero-China HS6 rows are not shipped to the browser.
  • Suggested citation: Helfrich, Ian (2026), Import Localization Atlas.

Scope and honesty note

Scores rest on dimensions computable from hard public data: import dependence, scale, and economic complexity. Three dimensions of the full framework—domestic value added, ESG, and policy alignment—need country-specific data and are left as documented inputs, not fabricated. Treat the Localize shortlist as a first screen, not a finished investment list. This tool is a synthesis-and-application; the two-axis typology is not claimed as novel. The novel VCC index and reverse-dependence round-trip are developed in separate work.
